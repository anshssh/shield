01 - INTRODUCTION

Addressing the Urban Waste Crisis

Municipal waste dumping is a **rising issue in urban areas**, often overwhelming existing infrastructure and sanitation efforts.

Our project proposes an IoT + AI-powered automated surveillance system that actively detects garbage dumping, records evidence, and instantly generates complaint reports.

THE CHALLENGE

Inefficient Manual Systems

  • ⇒

    Manual patrols offer **inconsistent coverage** and fail to provide 24/7 surveillance.

  • ⇒

    Lack of geo-located, time-stamped **irrefutable evidence** makes enforcement challenging.

  • ⇒

    High **manpower costs** for passive, round-the-clock surveillance duties.

02 - PROJECT OBJECTIVES

The Goals of Automation

1.

Auto-Complaint Generation

Automatically generate and forward complaint reports to municipal authorities.

2.

Run an AI model (YOLOv5) to reliably detect garbage-dumping actions.

Ensure prompt and reliable evidence upload using WiFi as primary and LTE as backup.

03 - METHODOLOGY IN DEPTH

The Integrated IoT + AI System

#1: Motion Detection & Video Recording

**Hardware:** PIR Sensor + Arduino Uno R3, and ESP32-CAM with microSD slot.

The **PIR sensor** is connected to the Arduino Uno to detect human movement near restricted garbage areas. Upon detection, the Arduino sends a HIGH signal, triggering the **ESP32-CAM** to record a **10-second video clip**. The clip is saved locally to the SD card with an onboard flash LED indicator for feedback.

#2: Connectivity and Geolocation

**Hardware:** SIM7670 LTE Module with GPS, utilizing AT commands.

The **SIM7670** provides two crucial functions: **GPS coordinates** for geo-tagging the evidence, and **LTE backup** connectivity. This ensures video metadata contains precise location and timestamp, enabling reliable uploads and offline scenario syncing (videos queued in SD and uploaded later).

#3: Server-Side Processing & AI Analysis

**Software:** Flask Server, Custom AI Model (YOLO/Action Recognition), Telegram Bot API.

The video is uploaded to the **Flask server**. The server runs the AI model to analyze the action. If a **garbage throwing action** is detected, the system auto-files a complaint, integrating with the **Telegram Bot** for instant notification to the admin channel with supporting evidence (photo, time, GPS).

#4: Dataset Creation & Model Training

**Resources:** Custom Video Dataset, LabelImg/Roboflow, YOLOv5, Google Colab (GPU).

We collected video samples of people throwing garbage, extracting and annotating frames using **LabelImg/Roboflow** in YOLO format. The custom dataset (positive/negative samples for person, garbage, throwing action) was used to train the **YOLOv5 model** on Google Colab, achieving an approximate **75–80% detection accuracy** on the exported `best.pt` weights.

04 & 05 - RESULTS & ANALYSIS

Prototype Validation and Findings

~80%

AI Accuracy

Achieved stable detection accuracy on the custom-trained YOLOv5 model.

LTE+WiFi

Reliable Connectivity

The WiFi–LTE fallback system successfully improved data transmission reliability.

SD Backup

Guaranteed Data Integrity

Local storage queuing ensured videos were never lost, even when offline.

Project Analysis and Insights

Strengths

Automated, reliable, scalable, and provides a clear audit trail for authorities.

Challenges

Initial challenges included network stability in remote areas, fine-tuning AI accuracy, and managing power needs.

Key Insight

Implementing the dedicated **WiFi-LTE fallback** with SD queuing was critical to achieving real-world deployment reliability.

06 - CONCLUSION

A Proven Solution for Smart Cities

The **AI-WasteGuard** system demonstrates a functional, end-to-end workflow: **Motion → Video → AI Analysis → Auto-Complaint**. This 24/7 automated monitoring system supports municipal waste enforcement, saves manpower, and aligns perfectly with the smart city vision.
